News from MECF!

In 2013, only one out of three children in fourth grade in New York State was able to read at the proficient or advanced level. The Maternity and Early Childhood Foundation is pleased to announce the receipt of a $5,000 grant from the J.M. McDonald Foundation for the "Read to Me" initiative. The  grant will be used to expand resources available to our MECF programs to encourage young parents to read and sing with their infants and young children.

MECF also received a $2,500 grant from the Maximus Foundation that will be used to upgrade our office's equipment to better serve our programs, as well as provide educational materials to our programs' mothers, infants, and young children. The Maximus Foundation is committed to supporting organizations that work with disadvantaged populations and underserved communities in the areas of health, child and family development, and community development, and MECF is thrilled to have their support!
At our annual Fall Luncheon the Board of Directors presented the 2014 Champion for New York's Children and Families Award to Gerald D. Jennings, former Mayor of Albany. Jennings is recognized for his visionary and progressive leadership as Mayor, particularly his steadfast devotion to ensuring all children, regardless of race or economic background, have the opportunity to grow up in a safe and nurturing environment in order to reach their full potential. Congratulations to Mayor Jennings on a well-deserved award! Many thanks to all who attended the Fall Luncheon for their generous support of MECF!
