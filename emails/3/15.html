 August 2014


Greetings All!
This month we have been busy visiting program sites across the state. It is so wonderful to stop in and see the inspiring work these programs are doing in their communities. Our travels this month brought us to Cornell Cooperative Extension Association of Jefferson County, North Country Family Health Center and Community Maternity Services of Warren and Washington Counties. We are so happy to work with these great programs and look forward to visiting the rest of our programs soon!
 
Meet Carson and his mom Kameron! Kameron just graduated from Queensbury High School and is enrolled in online classes at Hudson Valley Community College to begin this fall. She will work toward a degree in Early Childhood Education.


 
Meet Dalton at Cornell Cooperative Extension! Parents Melissa and Dom have worked closely with Kelly, their caseworker, to learn about caring for infants and positive parenting. Melissa participated in a peer mentoring program offered by Jefferson County Mental Health Association and was asked to speak at a recent statewide conference!
 
Melissa Herrara, LPN, demonstrates step by step how to correctly perform infant CPR if a baby stops breathing to a group of moms at North Country Family Health Center. One of the moms, due in mid-July, was thrilled to pick up a  new car seat!
 
Helpful Resources:
  • NYS Office of Children and Family Services serves New York's public by promoting the safety and well-being of our children and families. Several resources can be found on their website including: New York Loves Safe Babies Resource Sheet, Childcare for Infant or Toddler Checklist, and this Babysitter and Emergency Contact Checklist in English and Spanish
  • Office of Temporary and Disability Assistance is responsible for supervising programs that provide assistance and support to eligible families. Resources on their website include: A Driver's Manual for New Dads and Teen Parents and The Law Brochure
  • NYS Department of Health website provides tips for staying healthy, news stories, videos and photos that are distributed by NYSDOH. Resources include: Safe Sleep for Your Baby Flyer and Can Your Baby Hear You Screening Information
  • NYS Child and Adult Care Food Program through the Department of Health has created healthy infant meal plans which can be downloaded here and here.
